Biography

Lenny Kravitz was born in New York on May 26, 1964, to NBC TV News producer Sy Kravitz and famed actress Roxie Roker. His mother is African-American and Bahamian, and his father is Russian:Jewish. Ella Fitzgerald and Duke Ellington were among the many famous people he was surrounded by when he was a child. In his youth, Kravitz began learning different instruments and writing his own songs. Until the age of 10, he lived in New York, then moved to Los Angeles, California where he attended Beverly Hills High School with classmates such as Nicholas Cage and Slash. Fela Kuti, Bill Withers, Marvin Gaye, Pharoah Sanders, and Miles Davis were among his musical influences at the time. He was influenced by John Lennon and Bob Marley in later years.1989 marked the release of his first album, "Let Love Rule." Known for mixing rock, funk, reggae, hard rock, soul, and R&B, he creates a sound that is easily recognizable.

Kravitz met Lisa Bonet from the Cosby Show in 1985, and they began dating after two years. In November of 1987, they were married in Las Vegas. Bonet and Kravitz had a daughter on December 1, 1988, who became an actress, singer, and model. Bonet and Kravitz separated in 1991. A friendly divorce was finalized in 1993 between them.

In addition to his music career, Lenny Kravitz is also an accomplished photographer. Two times, he has partnered with Leica cameras. A Leica M:P edition entitled "The Correspondent" was released in 2015, followed by a Monochrom edition entitled "Drifter" in 2019. The Kravitz Design company was founded in 2003 by him as well. Morgans Hotel Group, Swarovski Crystal, and The Setai Group are among its clients. They do commercial, residential, and product creative direction.

As a 59:year:old vegan, Lenny maintains a healthy body. Known for living simplistically in his airstream trailer, he spends most of his time in the Bahamas. Additionally, he owns a townhouse in Paris, a mansion in Miami, and a gorgeous re:imagined coffee plantation in Brazil.

A nominee for the 2024 Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, he has sold over 40 million albums worldwide.
